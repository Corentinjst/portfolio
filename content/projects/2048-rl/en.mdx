## Context

Personal project aimed at training a **reinforcement learning** agent to play the game
**2048**. The goal: start from scratch and reach the 2048 tile (and beyond) autonomously,
by iterating on the model architecture, reward shaping and hyperparameters.

## Problem

2048 is a perfect information game with a very large state space (~3.5 × 10³⁰
possible configurations). The agent must learn non-trivial strategies: keeping
tiles ordered, maintaining an open board and planning chain merges, solely
from gameplay experience.

## Solution

Implementation of a complete training pipeline based on **MaskablePPO**
(Proximal Policy Optimization with invalid action masking):

- **Gymnasium environment**: the game board is encapsulated in an environment
  compatible with the standard RL ecosystem, with chained wrappers for preprocessing,
  reward shaping and action masking.
- **One-hot encoding**: each cell of the board is transformed into its power of 2 then
  one-hot encoded over 16 channels, producing a `(16, 4, 4)` tensor suited for
  convolutional processing.
- **Custom CNN**: a feature extractor with 2 convolutional layers captures the spatial
  patterns of the board (alignments, tile clusters), followed by separate heads for
  the actor and the critic.
- **Reward shaping**: composite reward integrating merges (superlinear),
  empty cells, board monotonicity and a survival bonus.
- **VecNormalize**: reward normalization to zero mean and unit variance,
  reducing value_loss by a factor of 68,000× and unlocking learning.

## Iterative Approach

Three configurations were developed and compared via 3M step runs:

- **V1** - Baseline PPO with linear reward: plateauing at 256-512, unstable value_loss.
- **V2** - Superlinear reward `log2(score)²`, LR schedule, wider critic: still
  stuck at 512 due to explosive value_loss (~2,153).
- **V3** - Addition of **VecNormalize** on rewards, reduction of n_epochs (10→4),
  increase of n_steps (2048→4096): value_loss brought down to 0.03, stable learning.
