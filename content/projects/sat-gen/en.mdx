## Context

**Computer Vision** project carried out in the final year of engineering school (ESILV A5).
The goal: generate **photorealistic satellite images** from simple segmentation masks indicating
roads and buildings. The model synthesizes images of **arbitrary size** without visible seams,
paving the way for the creation of synthetic data to train aerial detection models.

## Problem Statement

Annotated satellite images are scarce and expensive to produce. Generating realistic images
from segmentation masks would allow massive augmentation of existing datasets.
The main challenge is twofold: producing visually convincing images at the patch level (256×256),
then assembling them into large-scale images **without border artifacts**.

## Solution

Full **conditional Pix2Pix** (cGAN) pipeline with several innovations:

- **Dual-channel input**: roads (channel 0) and buildings (channel 1) are separated into two
  distinct channels, providing fine-grained control over generation.
- **U-Net Generator** (~54M parameters): 8 encoding/decoding blocks with skip connections,
  Instance Normalization and dropout kept at inference for stochastic diversity.
- **PatchGAN Discriminator**: 70×70 patch-level classification with Spectral Normalization
  for training stability.
- **Multi-layer Perceptual Loss**: VGG-19 feature extraction across 5 levels to
  preserve natural textures beyond simple pixel-to-pixel reconstruction.
- **Cosine-fade blending**: seamless patch assembly with 50% overlap and cosine
  transition, eliminating visible seams on large images.
- **SAM3 (Segment Anything Model)**: automatic generation of complementary masks
  to enrich existing annotations via weak supervision.

## Key Technical Choices

- **Instance Normalization** rather than BatchNorm to remove dependency on batch size
  and allow flexible inference.
- **Spectral Normalization** on the discriminator to prevent it from dominating training.
- **Label smoothing** (real=0.9) to prevent mode collapse.
- **Dropout at inference**: a deliberate choice specific to Pix2Pix, enabling stochastic
  variations to be generated on each call.
- **Cosine Annealing** of the learning rate with η_min = 1e-6 for stable training.
- **Multiple noise strategies** (Gaussian, Uniform, Sparse, Per-Channel) for empty mask
  areas, avoiding generation artifacts.

## Results

The model generates visually realistic satellite images with consistent textures for
roads, buildings, and vegetation. The seamless blending pipeline produces images of
arbitrary size without visible seams, validating the approach for large-scale synthetic
data generation.

The model is available on the **Hugging Face Hub** and accessible via a **FastAPI REST API**
as well as an **interactive web interface**.
