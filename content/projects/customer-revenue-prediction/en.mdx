## Context

Academic project (ESILV A4) focused on predicting transactional revenue per session on the
**Google Merchandise Store**. The dataset comes from **Google Analytics** and contains
~900,000 sessions with JSON-encoded columns.
The goal: predict `totalTransactionRevenue` for each session.

## Problem Statement

The dataset exhibits an **extreme class imbalance**: ~98.7% of sessions have zero revenue.
The raw columns are nested as multi-level JSON, with many missing values and categorical
variables with very high cardinality. The challenge is to extract a usable predictive signal
despite this heavily skewed distribution.

## Solution

A 3-stage iterative pipeline, from raw preprocessing to tuned model ensembling:

- **JSON Expansion**: recursive extraction of nested JSON columns (device,
  geoNetwork, totals, trafficSource, adwordsClickInfo) into usable tabular features.
- **Robust Preprocessing**: removal of "not available in demo dataset" columns,
  nullity-threshold dropping, multi-strategy imputation (most frequent, constant, median),
  cardinality reduction and one-hot encoding.
- **Feature Engineering**: visitor-level aggregates (sum/mean/std of pageviews),
  temporal features (local time via timezone mapping by sub-continent, day of week),
  GDP by country (World Bank data), monthly churn rate, traffic source consolidation
  and binary Google flags.
- **Target Transformation**: applying `log1p` to revenue to stabilize the
  distribution and facilitate learning.
- **Resampling**: experimentation with SMOTE and undersampling
  to address the imbalance; conclusion that the original data performs better.
- **User-level Prediction**: aggregating sessions by `fullVisitorId` to capture
  each visitor's overall behavior.

## Iterative Approach

Three stages of increasing complexity:

- **Stage 1**: Full preprocessing and baseline models: Random Forest, Linear
  Regression, LightGBM, SVR. Setting up a reproducible pipeline and EDA
  (correlations, PCA, distributions).
- **Stage 2**: Feature engineering (8 new features), before/after comparison
  across models. The new features account for **42.6% of importance** in
  the Random Forest. Introduction of ensembling techniques (Bagging, Stacking, Voting).
- **Stage 3**: Hyperparameter tuning via RandomizedSearchCV on the 4 best models,
  SMOTE/undersampling experimentation, user-level prediction, neural network (Keras MLP
  with architecture tuning), and final ensembling of tuned models.

The **StackingRegressor** combining Random Forest, XGBoost, LightGBM and ExtraTrees with
a LinearRegression meta-learner achieves the best performance. Feature engineering delivers
a significant gain across all models, and ensembling techniques help squeeze out the last
RMSE points. SMOTE/undersampling experimentation showed that these techniques degrade
performance on this type of distribution.
